A dashboard can expose waste. It cannot make uncertainty speakable.

That is why the Apollo Program is necessary, but insufficient. Measure flow. Remove waste. Tighten the machinery. Those tools work. I’ve used them. But they become theater when the real bottleneck is social: people are protecting status, identity, and production at the same time.

I saw it on a night paving job. Trucks stacked up. Idle time was burning dollars in the dark. The dashboard showed the waste, but the waste-creating decision had been made hours earlier, when uncertainty was still high and the only culturally admissible move was to over-order trucks.

The fix was not another lecture about efficiency. It was Ritual Time: a separate container for planning and review that made uncertainty admissible before the fact, and made it safe to be wrong in public after. AI will make optimization cheaper. It will not make truth easier to tell.
