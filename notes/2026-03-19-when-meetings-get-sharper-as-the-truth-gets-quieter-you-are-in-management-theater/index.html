Archetype Diagnosis·March 19, 2026

When meetings get sharper as the truth gets quieter, you are in Management Theater.

It appears quickly. The calendar fills with reviews, retros, and alignment sessions. The decks are polished. The language is careful. But the real decisions happen in side channels, in private, or in one person’s head. Everyone knows which truths are still unsafe.

What drives it is simple: leaders confuse ritual form with trust. They think process produces culture. So when disruption rises, they add more ceremony. More postmortems. More check-ins. More candor. But if the form outruns the human substrate, the ritual becomes a trap.

That is failed alchemy. Error is not being turned into better structure. It is being converted into caution, performance, and concealment.

Left untreated, each hollow ceremony burns a little more trust. The smart people stop speaking first. Then they stop caring. Then the company dies of its own process.
